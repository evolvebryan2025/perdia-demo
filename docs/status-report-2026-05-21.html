Update · Round 02

Every note from your May 19 review is fixed and live.

We pulled apart your nine comments on the Cybersecurity article and shipped a fix for each one. Here's what changed, in plain English.

9/9

Every comment addressed

Generation pipeline updated. New articles will follow the new rules from today onward.

What we fixed

Your nine notes, one at a time

✓
"Internal and external linking are not following rules and using short codes."

Links now go through GetEducated shortcodes

Articles can't be published until every link routes through the proper GetEducated shortcode format. Raw affiliate links are blocked at publish time.

✓
"The 'view more degrees' buttons are not driving the user to the same category and concentration as the short code calls for."

"View More Degrees" now lands on the right page

The button URL is now built from the same category and concentration as the shortcode itself — and the button is now actually clickable inside the preview.

✓
"These short codes for GE Picks can be improved; one could be at the bachelor's level and the other at the masters since the article talks about them both."

Two GE Picks blocks now show different degree levels

When an article discusses both bachelor's and master's, the two blocks split across those levels so the reader sees a wider range of schools.

✓
"For the Nursing article, it put in the cat and conc codes for Business Administration."

Topic matching can no longer silently default to the wrong subject

Added a confidence check. If the article clearly belongs to one field (nursing, healthcare, business, engineering, etc.) and the matched category doesn't fit, the system refuses the match and flags it for review instead of guessing.

✓
"There is a paragraph about bachelors and masters degree paths with no internal linking over to the associated cat/con/degree level URL."

Degree mentions in paragraphs now get internal links

When a paragraph mentions "bachelor's degree" or "master's degree", the first mention is automatically wrapped in a link to the matching GetEducated degree page.

✓
"No short code used for Ranking Report linking. Also the Ranking reports should be the best match content."

Best-match Ranking Reports added near the end

Each article now pulls the most relevant Ranking Reports by topic and degree level and links them through the proper shortcode under a "Related Ranking Reports" heading.

✓
"At the bottom of the page, this content block can be removed."

"How we researched and created this article" block removed

The bottom block with sources and timestamps is stripped from new articles by default. It can be turned back on with a single setting if you ever want it again.

✓
"This sentence doesn't properly reinforce the importance of accreditation."

Accreditation language now firm and consequence-driven

The AI is no longer allowed to hedge ("some employers may prefer…"). It must state the real consequences — loss of federal aid, non-transferable credits, ineligibility for licensure — and cite a real source like the Department of Education or CHEA.

✓
"In WP, a second category is not selected — a second category should also be selected based on the best fit for the content piece."

WordPress now gets a second category that matches the topic

Every article still publishes under "Articles" plus a topic-specific second category (cybersecurity, nursing, business, education, etc.) chosen automatically from the content.

All nine items closed. Live on the GetEducated workflow as of today.
